Sports

During PST I played a game of soccer in a nearby village with some teachers at a different school. In what might have been one of my best ever soccer performances, I racked up 2 goals (one was awesome) in our 3-1 win.

Football (soccer) is popular? – Who would have thought? I see jerseys everywhere. People play anywhere using anything. The students who play at my school are really good. I think they are the best out of the 885 boys aged 18-21 who attend Tosamaganga secondary. That’s why I play basketball instead so I can feel better about myself. Striking up conversations with strangers is made easier by the fact that there’s a good chance you can fall back on a soccer-themed conversation topic.

Balling out like Lebron – is something I look forward to most days. I play basketball with a group of students on the court nestled within school grounds. Not to sound cocky but it’s not uncommon to rack up a triple double. It does help that I’m about 70th percentile for height here…

Frisbee – is catching on quick! I started evening toss-arounds in early January. Since then, the number of interested students has only increased. A few of them want to start a club, and I’m not opposed in the slightest. Looks like something I’m going to end up doing.

Volleyball – is my last resort. If people aren’t playing frisbee, basketball, or soccer (in that order), I always can join in on a volleyball session. Having so many older boys who love sports is a really wonderful blessing and is helping me show face when I don’t feel particularly extroverted.
