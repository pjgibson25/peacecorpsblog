Cultural

While I'm sure some of the other thorns could fit in here, here are some cultural challenges.

Here is some of my trash / the trash that the volunteer before me left. One day, I will have to burn it. Today is not that day.

Gender Roles – This is probably the biggest thorn, and I’m a guy. I listen to female volunteers, but I’ll never fully understand what it must be like for them. I thought about making an entire page on this one. I think it would make me really sad if I did. I’m going to bullet some of the biggest challenges associated with gender roles. I definitely have not hit all of them, but this is what popped into my head.

  • Religion – Some say the bible says men are born to lead women
  • Cheating culture – People (mostly men I think) have multiple secret partners.
  • Talk to the man – If me and a female volunteer are together, people will often speak to me when my friend can clearly speak for herself
  • Bride Price – you pay the family of the bride to marry her. Can lead to less than consensual partnerships.
  • Abusive admiration – Chris Brown is the most popular American artist here. R. Kelly is up there. The most popular artist, Diamond Platinumz, has also been known to hit women.
  • Clothing – most all female volunteers can only wear shorts when in an entirely private setting, lest face judgment.
  • Marriage – people get married early and are criticized if they aren’t
  • The white woman – many male peers inquire me about hooking them up with American white women
  • Act differently – people just in general act very differently in front of men and women.
  • Professionalism in workplace – I get asked if I think other female teachers are attractive.
  • The worst one – At other schools, it is too frequent the case that a male teacher is having a sexual relationship with a young female student. The punishment for the teacher is typically moving schools or a demotion.
  • Role in house – fully expected to cook, clean, raise children.
  • Need to find Tanzanian woman – male peers want me to find a Tanzanian woman who will take care of me. Also that way, I will stay in Tanzania.

Animal cruelty – In general, dog’s are a whole lot meaner here, partially because humans aren’t that nice to them. Little kids regularly throw sticks or small rocks at dogs/cats.

Personal space – You will touch the person on the bus next to you. If you are in line, someone is likely grazing your butt because they are so close. Maybe I just have a nice butt.

Drop toilets – Because nearly all bathrooms are drop toilets, and these aren’t quite as sanitary as western toilets, bathrooms smell pretty bad. Bathrooms smell really bad. You get used to it though. I think I might cry when I use a McDonalds bathroom and it smells nicer than the one in my house.

Garbage fires – There’s no garbage truck that drives around. Therefore, there’s lots of litter and people remove their own garbage by burning it. The first time the smoke wafted into my home-stay room, I immediately got sick. It has to have countless negative health effects and I get sad when I see children playing around these fires.

Betting – I’m sure it’s a challenge I just haven’t seen as much of in the states, but sports betting is wildly popular. I fear that many people lose lots of money from this habit.

Unhealthy alcohol consumption – Again, I’m sure it’s a challenge I just haven’t seen as much of in the states, but it seems more socially acceptable to drink very frequently. People who don’t are often viewed as not cool.

Wealth – Many students pick careers on the sole basis of making lots of money. Again, sure this happens in America.

Self Deprecation – There’s a common theme of people wanting to leave Tanzania to go to America because “everything is better there”. I wish they could see some of the lovely things I see in Tanzania.
